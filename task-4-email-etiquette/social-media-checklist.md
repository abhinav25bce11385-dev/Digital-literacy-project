# Social Media Checklist for College Students: Do's and Don'ts

> Your digital presence matters more than you think. Here's a practical guide to keeping it clean, safe, and future-ready.

---

## ✅ 5 Things You Should Definitely Be Doing

### 1. DO check your privacy settings — and keep checking them
Platforms update their policies all the time. Make it a habit to revisit who can actually see your posts, photos, and personal info.

### 2. DO pause before you post
The internet has a long memory. A quick gut-check — *"Would I be okay if my professor or a future employer saw this?"* — can save you a lot of regret later.

### 3. DO verify before you share
Misinformation spreads fast. Before you retweet or repost something, take 30 seconds to fact-check it. Your credibility is worth more than a viral moment.

### 4. DO build your presence intentionally
LinkedIn isn't just for job hunters. Start connecting with peers, alumni, and people in your field now — your future self will thank you.

### 5. DO treat people online the way you would in person
Disagreements happen, but basic respect goes a long way. How you engage online says a lot about who you are.

---

## ❌ 5 Things You Really Need to Stop Doing

### 1. DON'T put your personal details out there publicly
Your address, phone number, daily routine, financial stuff — none of that belongs on a public feed. It's not paranoia, it's just smart.

### 2. DON'T post content that could come back to haunt you
Photos from that party, videos of questionable situations — these can violate your university's code of conduct and follow you for years.

### 3. DON'T vent about your professors, classmates, or internship online
It feels satisfying in the moment, but it rarely ends well. What seems like a private rant can very quickly become a very public problem.

### 4. DON'T get pulled into toxic arguments
Online fights almost never lead anywhere good. Walk away from heated threads — screenshots exist forever, and context gets lost fast.

### 5. DON'T trust the delete button to save you
Deleting a post doesn't erase it. If even one person saw it before you hit delete, it lives on — in a screenshot, an archive, or someone's memory.

---

*Your digital footprint is being built right now, whether you're being intentional about it or not. Make it one you're proud of.*
