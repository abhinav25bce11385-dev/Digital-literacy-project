# Stay Safe Online: Cybersecurity Checklist for College Students

> Let's be real — college students are one of the most targeted groups online. Between new bank accounts, job hunting, and living independently for the first time, there's a lot that scammers can exploit. This checklist won't take long to go through, but it might save you a lot of grief.

---

##  Financial & UPI Safety

- [ ] **Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money**
  Your PIN only does one thing — authorise a payment *from* you. If someone's asking you to enter it to "receive" funds, stop. That's not how it works. That's a scam.

- [ ] **Always check who you're paying before you confirm**
  Before entering your PIN, look at the name on the screen. Whether it's Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm — one second of verification is all it takes to avoid sending money to the wrong person.

- [ ] **No real job will ask you to pay first**
  If an internship or part-time opportunity asks for a "registration fee," "security deposit," or "equipment charge" via UPI before you've even started — walk away. Legitimate employers simply don't do this.

---

##  Account & Identity Protection

- [ ] **Switch on Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) everywhere**
  Your university email, WhatsApp, LinkedIn, banking apps — all of it. 2FA is one of the simplest things you can do, and it makes a huge difference if your password ever gets compromised.

- [ ] **Go private on social media — and mean it**
  Your phone number, home address, and college ID have no business being on a public profile. Scammers piece together information from multiple sources. Don't make it easy for them.

- [ ] **Stop reusing the same password**
  Your pet's name with a "1" at the end isn't a password — it's an invitation. Use a password manager to generate and store strong, unique passwords for every platform you use.

---

##  Safe Browsing & Device Hygiene

- [ ] **Never do banking on public Wi-Fi**
  Free Wi-Fi at cafés, airports, or campus lounges is convenient — and risky. If you need to pay fees, shop online, or check your bank account, switch to mobile data instead.

- [ ] **Be very skeptical of WhatsApp forwards**
  "Free premium software," "leaked exam papers," "government laptop scheme" — if it sounds too good to be true in a group chat, it almost certainly is. These links are designed to steal your credentials or install malware quietly in the background.

- [ ] **Keep your devices updated**
  It's easy to keep dismissing those update notifications. Don't. Software updates patch real security vulnerabilities. This includes your phone's OS, your laptop, and your antivirus.

---

##  If Something Goes Wrong — Act Fast

Don't wait. The sooner you report cyber fraud, the better your chances of limiting the damage.

*Staying safe online isn't about being paranoid — it's about being informed. A few good habits now can protect your money, your identity, and your peace of mind throughout college and beyond.*
